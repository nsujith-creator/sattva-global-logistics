Not guaranteed export growth.

Lower tariffs improve competitiveness. They do not replace product quality, certification capacity, buyer networks, pricing discipline or supply-chain execution.

Commercial reading

The agreement changes the duty equation. It does not complete the export transaction.

Indian manufacturers may gain a meaningful price advantage in the UK market. But the advantage can disappear if the shipment is classified incorrectly, fails its origin test, misses a certification requirement or carries weak freight and destination-cost planning.

For exporters, the practical sequence is simple: confirm eligibility first, calculate the full landed cost second, and only then commit price and delivery terms to the buyer.

You are not selling lower duty alone. You are selling a compliant, correctly costed and reliably executed UK supply proposition.

Frequently asked questions

What exporters should not assume

Are all Indian exports to the UK now duty-free?

No. The headline access is broad, but the applicable rate is product-specific. Confirm the exact HS classification, the UK tariff concession, the product-specific rule of origin and the required evidence before pricing the shipment on a zero-duty assumption.

What should I verify first?

Start with the exact HS code and product description. Then test the rule of origin using the product composition, manufacturing process and source of key inputs. Do not begin with the freight quote and work backwards.

Does CETA remove UK certification and labelling requirements?

No. Tariff preferences do not remove product safety, labelling, SPS, traceability, technical, environmental or sector-specific approval requirements.

Is a Certificate of Origin always enough?

No. The origin claim must be supported by the actual product facts and records. The accepted document or declaration format and supporting evidence should be confirmed for the product and shipment.

Can Sattva confirm whether my product legally qualifies?

Sattva can coordinate freight, documentation and execution around the verified treatment. Final classification, origin eligibility and customs treatment should be confirmed with the competent customs, certification or professional advisory authority.
